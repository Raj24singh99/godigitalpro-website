# EdTech Cold Email — 3 Angles
> Use these as the `notes` campaign directive in the CRM sheet, or as manual templates.
> The AI system will personalize from the lead's website + these directives.
> Pick one angle per batch of leads — don't mix angles in same batch.

---

## Angle 1 — Admission Funnel Leaks
*Best for: EdTech brands running ads, getting inquiries, but low enrollment conversion*

**Subject options (pick one):**
- `Admissions inquiry gaps costing enrollments`
- `Why your admission funnel is leaking`
- `The inquiry-to-enrollment gap most EdTech brands ignore`

**Notes directive for CRM:**
```
campaign: edtech_admission_funnel | angle: most edtech brands lose 60-70% of inquiries before an admissions call — the gap is landing page friction, slow response, and no follow-up system | audience: edtech founder or CMO or admissions head | pain_points: high inquiry drop-off, low inquiry-to-enrollment conversion, slow lead response time, generic landing pages | outcomes: more inquiries converting to admissions calls, lower CPL, higher enrollment rate | services: Google Ads + landing page CRO + lead nurturing + CRM follow-up sequence | offer: free 20-minute admission funnel audit | cta: book a 20-minute admission funnel audit | tone: consultative and specific, founder-to-founder | subject_style: pain-point + outcome | proof: 250 crore ad spend managed, 1000 crore revenue delivered
```

**Manual template (if not using AI generation):**
```
Hi [Name],

Most EdTech brands I've audited are running ads and getting inquiries — but losing 60–70% of them before an admissions call ever happens. The gap is almost always in three places: landing page friction, slow lead response time, and no structured follow-up system for unconverted inquiries.

I run GoDigitalPro. We help EdTech founders close this specific gap — better intent targeting, higher-converting pages, and a lead nurturing sequence that moves students to action.

Happy to share 2–3 specific observations about [Company]'s current setup if you're open to a quick 20-minute audit call.

If useful, you can book a quick strategy call:
[calendly link]
```

## Angle 2 — CPL / Ad Spend Waste
*Best for: EdTech brands actively spending on Google/Meta ads, CPL feels high*

**Subject options (pick one):**
- `Ad spend waste in your student acquisition funnel`
- `Cutting EdTech CPL without cutting reach`
- `Your CPL vs what it could be`

**Notes directive for CRM:**
```
campaign: edtech_cpl_waste | angle: edtech brands typically spend 40-60% of ad budget on audiences that will never enroll — not bad targeting, but the funnel after the click is not built for conversion | audience: edtech founder or performance marketing lead | pain_points: high CPL, poor ROAS on Google and Meta, broad unqualified traffic, high bounce on landing pages | outcomes: 25-40% CPL reduction, better quality leads, more enrollment per rupee spent | services: Google Ads restructure + Meta Ads targeting + landing page CRO + conversion tracking | offer: free paid funnel audit — no fluff, just specific numbers and fixes | cta: book a 20-minute paid funnel audit call | tone: direct and data-driven, peer-to-peer | subject_style: outcome-led, specific number or stat | proof: 250 crore ad spend managed across edtech and other verticals
```

**Manual template:**
```
Hi [Name],

EdTech brands typically burn 40–60% of their ad budget on audiences that will never enroll. It's not always bad targeting — more often it's that the funnel after the click isn't built for conversion. High-bounce landing pages, no lead scoring, slow follow-up.

We work with EdTech founders on fixing exactly this: tighter intent signals in targeting, faster-loading pages, and a nurturing sequence that converts more of the same traffic into enrolled students.

If your CPL feels high relative to enrollment numbers, worth a quick 20-minute look at your setup.
```

## Angle 3 — Organic / SEO Gap
*Best for: EdTech brands relying 100% on paid, no organic presence*

**Subject options (pick one):**
- `SEO gaps reducing qualified student inquiries`
- `Why students searching for your courses can't find you`
- `Organic channel you're leaving untouched`

**Notes directive for CRM:**
```
campaign: edtech_seo | angle: most edtech brands are nearly invisible for the course searches their students actually use — paid fills the gap short term but at high cost — structured SEO compounds inbound at fraction of paid cost | audience: edtech founder or growth lead | pain_points: 100% dependency on paid ads, high CAC, no organic traffic, weak course page SEO, no content strategy | outcomes: compounding organic student inquiries, lower overall CAC, less dependency on ad spend | services: EdTech SEO strategy + course landing page optimization + content cluster + technical SEO | offer: free SEO gap audit — show exactly which keywords and pages to target first | cta: book a 20-minute SEO audit call | tone: strategic and forward-looking | subject_style: gap identification + opportunity framing | proof: 250 crore ad spend managed — seen the paid ceiling, built organic to complement it
```

**Manual template:**
```
Hi [Name],

Most EdTech brands I review are nearly invisible for the exact course searches their students use. Google Ads fills the gap short-term, but at high cost — and once you pause spend, traffic drops to zero.

A structured SEO strategy — proper course category pages, long-tail content targeting student intent, technical fixes — can build compounding inbound at a fraction of paid cost. We've helped EdTech brands build this organic foundation alongside performance campaigns.

Happy to show exactly where [Company] has the biggest organic opportunity — takes about 20 minutes.
```

## Notes on Using These Angles
- **One angle per outreach batch** — mixing angles in one batch makes it hard to measure what works
- **Track reply rates per angle** in the CRM `notes` or a separate sheet tab
- **After 30 leads per angle**, compare reply rates and double down on the best performer
- **Personalization boost**: if you have the lead's website, the AI system will pull specific observations from it — the angle + website combo is much stronger than angle alone
